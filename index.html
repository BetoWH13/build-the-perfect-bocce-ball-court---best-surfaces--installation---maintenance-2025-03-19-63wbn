Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ideal size for a bocce court?

A regulation bocce court measures 13.1ft wide by 91.9ft long (4m x 28m). However, recreational courts can be built smaller, with a minimum recommended size of 10ft x 60ft for enjoyable gameplay.

What's the best surface material for a bocce court?

The most popular and recommended surface is crushed oyster shells or stone dust over a properly prepared base. These materials provide excellent ball roll, proper speed, and good drainage while being relatively low maintenance.

How long does it take to build a court?

With proper planning and preparation, a basic court can be constructed in 3-5 days. However, more complex installations with advanced drainage systems and amenities may take 1-2 weeks.

What kind of maintenance is required?

Regular maintenance includes weekly brushing of the playing surface, monthly leveling checks, and seasonal deep cleaning. The court should also be rolled periodically to maintain proper compaction.

What's the average cost to build a court?

Costs typically range from $6,000 to $12,000 for a basic court, depending on size and materials. Premium installations with lighting, drainage systems, and seating areas can range from $15,000 to $30,000.
